SpaceX opens the largest investor roadshow in history — targeting a $1.75 trillion valuation on Nasdaq

A 21-bank syndicate led by Goldman Sachs is now formally presenting the company to institutions, with pricing expected June 12 and a raise of up to $75 billion.

Elon Musk's SpaceX launched its investor roadshow on June 4, pursuing what would be the biggest public listing ever completed — eclipsing Saudi Aramco's 2019 record. At the midpoint of its projected valuation range of $1.75–2 trillion, the offering would set a new benchmark for capital formation in the technology sector. The company has reported annualised run-rate revenue exceeding $44 billion, driven by Starlink's 7.5 million global subscribers and a dense commercial launch manifest. Morningstar's internal valuation sits at $780 billion, suggesting the company is pricing at a meaningful premium to fundamentals — a wager on long-duration optionality in satellite internet and Mars colonisation.

The roadshow opens the same week Anthropic filed a confidential IPO prospectus at $965 billion, and as OpenAI targets a September debut. Together the three listings represent more than $3 trillion in anticipated market capitalisation — a scale not seen since the Dot-com peak.

Deep Analysis · AI & Capital Markets

The AI IPO wave is rewriting the rules of capital formation

Three companies — SpaceX, Anthropic, and OpenAI — are approaching public markets within the same six-month window, together representing more than $3 trillion in anticipated market capitalisation. No comparable cluster of mega-cap debuts has occurred since the 2000 dot-com peak. The structural implication for portfolio allocation is significant: institutional desks managing exposure to all three simultaneously are facing capacity constraints that are suppressing appetite for adjacent offerings. Crypto-native IPOs — Kraken, Ledger, Consensys — have all deferred to 2027. Meanwhile, Goldman Sachs projects total 2026 US IPO proceeds could reach $160 billion, the highest since 2021, driven almost entirely by AI and space technology listings.
